Featured Project

LLM Prompt Engineering & Annotation (Child-Adult Dialogues)

In this project, I developed and refined a prompt engineering framework to improve a Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite model's ability to annotate cognitive learning strategies in child-adult dialogues. By grounding the prompts in linguistic theory and iteratively debugging the logic, I improved annotation accuracy from 77.4% to 82.6%.

This work represents the first stage of a larger pipeline. Later stages, including supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and additional model evaluation, will be carried out by the research team.

  • Integrated Rumelhart & Norman's (1978) learning theory into the prompt framework, creating precise definitions and examples that enabled the model to distinguish between cognitive strategies like Accretion, Tuning, and Restructuring
  • Iteratively refined Python-based prompt logic in Google Colab to mitigate model errors, including factual inconsistencies, incomplete outputs, and over-tagging
  • Created examples and rule-based annotation guidelines with positive and negative examples to steer model behaviour, resolving logical contradictions and improving performance on nuanced cases, eg. distinguishing confirmation questions from information-seeking questions
  • Added a human-scoring field to the required JSON output format to enable manual validation of the LLM's annotations
